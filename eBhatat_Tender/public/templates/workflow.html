How eBharat Tender Works

The eBharat Tender system is a structured electronic procurement platform designed to ensure transparency, fairness, accountability, and efficiency in government purchasing and contract allocation.

1. Vendor Registration

Every supplier, contractor, or service provider must first create an account on the portal. During registration, the vendor provides legally valid business details including company registration certificate, GST number, PAN number, contact information, and bank details. Supporting documents are uploaded digitally for authentication.

This step ensures that only genuine and legally recognized businesses participate in the procurement process.

2. Document Verification and Approval

After submission, the concerned authority carefully verifies all uploaded documents. The verification process checks business authenticity, tax compliance, and required certifications. If all documents meet the requirements, the vendor account is approved.

If discrepancies are found, the vendor is notified to correct and resubmit the documents. This maintains transparency and system integrity.

3. Tender Publication by Government Departments

Government departments publish tenders on the portal with complete details such as scope of work, eligibility criteria, estimated project cost, Earnest Money Deposit (EMD), technical specifications, and submission deadlines.

Each tender is assigned a unique reference number for tracking and record-keeping.

4. Tender Document Download

Registered vendors can download tender documents to review project requirements carefully. The documents provide detailed instructions, contract terms, evaluation criteria, and compliance conditions.

Vendors must ensure they meet all eligibility conditions before proceeding.

5. Online Bid Submission

Vendors prepare and submit their bids electronically before the deadline. The bid typically includes two parts:

  • Technical Proposal – Documents proving capability and compliance.
  • Financial Proposal – Price quotation and cost breakdown.

The system encrypts submitted bids to ensure confidentiality until the official opening date.

6. Bid Opening and Evaluation

On the scheduled date, bids are opened electronically in the presence of authorized officials. Technical proposals are evaluated first to ensure compliance with requirements.

Only technically qualified bidders proceed to financial evaluation. The evaluation process follows predefined criteria to maintain fairness.

7. Award of Contract

After evaluation, the contract is awarded to the most suitable bidder based on technical merit and competitive pricing. The selected vendor receives an official notification.

Award details are published on the portal for public transparency.

8. Contract Execution and Monitoring

Once awarded, the vendor executes the contract as per agreed terms. The department monitors progress to ensure quality and timely completion.

Payment is processed according to contract milestones and performance verification.

Conclusion: The eBharat Tender platform ensures a transparent, structured, and accountable procurement process, reducing manual errors and promoting equal opportunity for all registered vendors.
